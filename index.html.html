The Art of Darkness

"Darkness is not the absence of light — it is the space where desire is born, where memory dissolves into sensation, where a single breath can alter everything."

NOIR was conceived in the candlelit ateliers of Grasse, where master perfumer Élise Fontaine spent seven years composing a fragrance that captures the precise moment when dusk surrenders to night. Each bottle contains 312 hand-selected botanical extracts, calibrated to evolve through three acts over twelve hours on the skin.

A Century of Shadow

Born from Obsession,
Refined by Time

In 1923, Jules Fontaine returned from the Orient carrying a small vial of Cambodian oud that had taken three decades to mature. He declared it would form the backbone of a fragrance unlike anything the Western world had known — a scent that would take another twenty years to perfect.
